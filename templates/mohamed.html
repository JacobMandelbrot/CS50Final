Why Code
Have you ever wondered how the game Flappy bird was made? How about websites like Google or Facebook? Perhaps other apps on your phone like a calculator or maybe instagram? Whether or not you have an idea how to make any of these projects, you could probably guess that the people who did knew how to code. That is the beautiful thing about programming. If you can think of it, you can probably code it!

There are many other reasons why coding is useful
Learn How to Think
“Everybody in this country should learn how to program a computer, because it teaches you how to think.” — Steve Jobs

Indeed, research has shown that students who learn how to code early on perform better coding tasks and other cognitive skills, such as creative thinking, mathematical skills, metacognition, and reasoning

Secure Job Opportunities
According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for jobs in technology was $97,430 in May 2021, which is more than twice the median annual wage ($45,760) overall.

Additionally, employment in this field is expected to grow 15% over the next 10 years, exceeding the national average.
Join a community of creators
Sharing is a big part of the coding process. By sharing your creations with friends and family, you can reinforces what you have learned and gain confidence.

Additionally, you can meet programmers from around the world by asking questions, collaborating on projects, and uploading your creations for the world to enjoy!
